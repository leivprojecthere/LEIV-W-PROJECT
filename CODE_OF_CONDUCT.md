# Code of Conduct – LEIV‑W Project

The LEIV‑W Project is an open‑source framework for **legal OSINT research** and **anonymous intelligence contributions**.  
To maintain credibility, privacy, and ethical standards, all contributors must follow these rules.

---

## Principles
- Respect → No harassment, hate speech, or personal attacks.  
- Privacy → No Personally Identifiable Information (PII), doxxing, or targeting individuals.  
- Integrity → Submissions must be authentic. **False or fabricated intel will be deleted and rejected.**  
- Responsibility → Contributions must comply with international cybercrime frameworks (IC3, FBI, INTERPOL).  
- Transparency → All submissions must clearly state their purpose and scope.  

---

## Contribution Guidelines
- Use **burner GitHub accounts** for submissions.  
- Delete your burner account after submitting to protect your privacy.  
- Contributions must focus on **research, OSINT workflows, anonymized intel, or tools**.  
- No illegal exploitation, malware, or weaponized code.  
- Respect repo structure — submit files to the correct directories (`/intel`, `/docs`, `/tools`, `/opsec`).  

---

## Moderation Rules
- Non‑compliant submissions will be rejected.  
- False intel or fabricated data will be **permanently deleted**.  
- Repeat violations may result in bans from contributing.  
- Maintainers have final authority on moderation decisions.  

---

## Enforcement
Violations of this Code of Conduct will result in:  
1. Warning → Notification of the issue.  
2. Rejection → Removal of non‑compliant content.  
3. Ban → Permanent exclusion from contributing if violations continue.  

---

## Contact
For moderation or conduct concerns, contact the maintainers via secure channels.  
This project prioritizes **privacy, legality, and ethical research** above all else.
